Taster Tool

Type in a taster name and browse their sensory abilities. Open a second window to comare two people directly. What do these plots mean?

The y-axis scores are adjusted averages to reward tasters who have experienced spikes more often than others. There is no perfect 100% adjusted average. Purple Senistivity violins indicate a taster who has gotten every spike of that compound correct.

Shows the bias (blue), majority vote rate (purple), and their respective means both for the individual and all tasters (black and gray) over time in monthly and yearly intervals. Note that the year averages are located directly above the x-axis year mark. For example the year average for 2017 is noted directly above the 17 and accounts for the monthly points plotted between the 17 and 18. Also take note that the y-axis is limited to 30%.

This plot is most useful to gain insight on a taster's change over time and learn if the taster's tendencies may need addressing.

Note: As bias increases the odds that majority vote rate will also increase.

Trustworthiness takes into account a tasters' bias (how often they vote Not TTB), majority vote rate (when they do vote Not TTB do others agree), and experience (how often they have tasted). A higher Trustworthiness score indicates a better taster.

This plot is most useful to gain insight on the strength of a taster at a quick glance.

Note: that this plot doesn't nessisarily have the same set of taster names as the other two. Double Check the names on the plots to avoid confusions.
